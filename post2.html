Blogging About Usable Design Analysis

Excited to share my thoughts and findings about "Useable Design Analysis."

Posted by Aric Masih on April 14, 2019

Discount Tire: Select your tire..

I am recently in the market for replacement tires for my vehicle. I am going to use Discount Tire site to search for tires and I am going to focus on tire search feature specifily on this site. Navigation elements on homepage are very clear and just easy to spot on sub menus hierarchies.

Usability

Purpose of this site is to help customers serach for tires by vehicle model, size, brand, and also place pruchase orders. I am using interface elements such as input controls, navigation, and informational compnents for usability.


Input Controls

Input controls consists of dropdown menu and text feilds which are easy to follow. You need to have basic information about your car's wheel specifications so you can selset from the given options.


Navigation

Navigation is rather smooth as you make on screen selection you move forward and follow the process to complete purchase order.


Breadcrumbs

As you start the prcosess from main page you have the navigation bar at the top and it stays fixed as you scroll up and down the page. After selecting the right sepcifications you move forward in the process but you have the link to navigation menu on the top in case you need to go back or cancle.


Competition

I picked Costco tire for comparision since they are selling similar products but have different usability elements.
